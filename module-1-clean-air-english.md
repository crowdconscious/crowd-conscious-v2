## Week 1: Understanding Our Air

### Lesson 1.1: "The Invisible Impact"
**Story Segment:** Mar√≠a downloads an air quality app and is shocked to see her neighborhood rated "Unhealthy." She shows it to Carlos, the new sustainability manager, who admits he never thought to check.

**Learning Content:**

**What is Air Quality?**
Air quality measures the concentration of pollutants that affect human health and the environment. The main indicators are:

- **PM2.5** (Particulate Matter): Tiny particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers that penetrate deep into lungs
  - *Safe level:* Below 12 ¬µg/m¬≥ (WHO standard)
  - *Unhealthy:* Above 35 ¬µg/m¬≥
  - *Real example:* Mexico City averages 25-30 ¬µg/m¬≥, above safe levels

- **PM10**: Larger particles that cause respiratory issues
  - *Safe level:* Below 50 ¬µg/m¬≥
  - *Sources:* Construction dust, industrial processes, unpaved roads

- **CO2** (Carbon Dioxide): Greenhouse gas from combustion
  - *Indoor safe level:* Below 1,000 ppm
  - *Outdoor concentration:* Currently ~420 ppm globally (up from 280 ppm pre-industrial)
  - *Critical threshold:* 1.5¬∞C warming linked to 450 ppm

- **VOCs** (Volatile Organic Compounds): Chemicals that evaporate into air
  - *Common sources:* Paints, solvents, cleaning products, plastics
  - *Health effects:* Headaches, dizziness, liver damage (long-term)

- **NOx & SOx**: Nitrogen and sulfur oxides from fuel combustion
  - *Main source:* Diesel trucks, industrial boilers
  - *Effects:* Acid rain, respiratory disease, smog

**Real-World Example: Grupo Bimbo's Air Quality Initiative**
In 2020, Grupo Bimbo committed to reducing absolute Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 50% by 2030. They:
- Installed air quality monitors in 12 manufacturing plants
- Switched 4,500 delivery vehicles to electric or hybrid
- Reduced PM2.5 emissions by 38% in urban facilities
- Saved $2.8M MXN annually in fuel costs

**ESG Connection:**
Air quality directly impacts:
- **E (Environmental):** Climate change mitigation, ecosystem health
- **S (Social):** Employee health, community relations, public health
- **G (Governance):** Regulatory compliance, risk management

## Week 2: Solutions That Work

### Lesson 2.1: "Quick Wins & Long-Term Strategy"
**Story Segment:** Carlos presents a plan to management. "We can start seeing results in 30 days," he says, showing cost savings projections. Mar√≠a volunteers her team to help implement.

**Learning Content:**

**The 3-Horizon Approach to Air Quality**

**Horizon 1: Immediate Actions (0-3 months) - Low/No Cost**

These deliver quick results and build momentum:

1. **No-Idling Policy**
   - Turn off vehicles after 2 minutes of stopping
   - *Impact:* Save 10-15% on fuel, reduce NOx by up to 80% in loading areas
   - *Cost:* $0 (signage only)
   - *Example:* Walmart Mexico implemented this and saved $4.2M MXN/year across distribution centers

2. **Lighting to LED**
   - Replace incandescent/fluorescent with LED
   - *Impact:* 60-80% less energy, less heat = less AC needed
   - *Payback:* 6-18 months
   - *Example:* Cemex converted 15,000 fixtures, saving 12 GWh/year (5,496 tons CO2)

3. **Smart Thermostat Installation**
   - Program HVAC to reduce usage during low-occupancy
   - *Impact:* 10-15% reduction in heating/cooling energy
   - *Cost:* $1,500-5,000 MXN per zone
   - *ROI:* 12-24 months

4. **Compressed Air Leak Fixes**
   - Identify and repair air leaks in production
   - *Impact:* 20-30% of compressed air is typically lost to leaks
   - *Cost:* Labor only ($5,000-15,000 MXN)
   - *Example:* A Guadalajara automotive parts factory saved $180,000 MXN/year

5. **Carpool Program**
   - Digital carpool matching for employees
   - Preferred parking for carpools
   - *Impact:* 15-25% reduction in commute emissions
   - *Cost:* Free using WhatsApp groups or apps

**Horizon 2: Medium-Term Investments (3-12 months) - Moderate Cost**

1. **Solar Panel Installation**
   - Rooftop or parking lot canopy solar
   - *Cost:* $15,000-25,000 MXN per kW installed
   - *Payback:* 3-5 years in Mexico
   - *Impact:* Reduce Scope 2 emissions by 30-100%
   
   **Real Example: Toyota Baja California**
   - Installed 5 MW solar array on factory roof
   - Generates 40% of facility electricity
   - Reduced annual CO2 by 3,800 tons
   - 4.2-year payback period
   - Qualified for government incentives (FIDE program)

2. **Electric Forklift Conversion**
   - Replace propane forklifts with electric
   - *Cost:* $200,000-350,000 MXN per forklift
   - *Savings:* 60-70% lower operating cost, zero indoor emissions
   - *Benefit:* Better indoor air quality, quieter operation

3. **High-Efficiency Boiler Upgrade**
   - Replace old boilers with 95%+ efficiency models
   - *Cost:* $500,000-2,000,000 MXN depending on size
   - *Savings:* 20-40% on gas consumption
   - *Payback:* 3-7 years

4. **Air Filtration & Ventilation Upgrade**
   - HEPA filters, improved exhaust systems
   - *Cost:* $50,000-500,000 MXN depending on facility
   - *Impact:* 80-95% reduction in indoor particulate matter
   
   **Real Example: Samsung Electronics Quer√©taro**
   - Installed advanced air filtration in cleanrooms
   - Reduced PM2.5 from 45 ¬µg/m¬≥ to 8 ¬µg/m¬≥
   - Worker sick days decreased by 32%
   - Productivity increased 7% (fewer errors due to better health)

**Horizon 3: Transformational Changes (1-3 years) - Major Investment**

1. **Fleet Electrification**
   - Replace diesel/gas vehicles with electric
   - *Cost:* Varies widely (vans: $800,000 MXN, trucks: $2,500,000 MXN)
   - *Operating savings:* 60-70% vs. diesel
   - *Government incentives:* Mexico offers up to 20% subsidy

2. **Renewable Energy Contract (PPA)**
   - Power Purchase Agreement for wind/solar
   - *Cost:* Typically no upfront cost
   - *Benefit:* Lock in 15-25 year fixed electricity rate
   - *Impact:* 100% renewable electricity (Scope 2 = 0)

3. **Process Electrification**
   - Replace gas heating with electric (heat pumps, induction)
   - *Cost:* $1,000,000-10,000,000 MXN depending on process
   - *Benefit:* Eliminate on-site combustion, highly efficient

**ESG Reporting Standards for Air Quality:**

Companies should report using:
- **GRI 305:** Emissions standard (Scope 1, 2, 3)
- **CDP Climate:** Global disclosure system
- **TCFD:** Climate-related financial disclosures
- **SASB:** Industry-specific sustainability metrics

## Week 3: Measurement & Mini-Project

### Lesson 3.1: "Measuring What Matters"
**Story Segment:** Three weeks after installing air monitors, Mar√≠a checks her phone. PM2.5 is down from 35 to 22. Her daughter hasn't needed her inhaler in 10 days. "Small changes add up," Don Roberto says, smiling.

**Learning Content:**

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for Air Quality:**

**Environmental KPIs:**
1. **Absolute Emissions**
   - Total tons CO2e/year (all scopes)
   - Track month-over-month changes
   - Target: 5-10% annual reduction

2. **Emissions Intensity**
   - kg CO2e per unit produced
   - kg CO2e per employee
   - kg CO2e per revenue (ton CO2e/$1M MXN)
   - *Why important:* Shows efficiency even as business grows

3. **Air Quality Index (AQI)**
   - Average daily AQI at facility boundary
   - Peak AQI readings
   - Days above WHO guidelines
   - Target: 90% of days in "Good" range (AQI < 50)

4. **Renewable Energy %**
   - % of electricity from renewable sources
   - Target: 50% by Year 3, 100% by Year 5 (global best practice)

**Social KPIs:**
1. **Employee Health Metrics**
   - Respiratory sick days per 100 employees
   - Air quality satisfaction score (survey 1-10)
   - Voluntary participation in green initiatives

2. **Community Relations**
   - Number of air quality complaints received
   - Community satisfaction score
   - Joint projects completed

**Financial KPIs:**
1. **Cost Savings**
   - Energy cost reduction (%)
   - Fuel cost reduction (%)
   - Avoided fines/penalties
   - Avoided health costs (estimated)

2. **Return on Investment**
   - Payback period for efficiency upgrades
   - NPV of emissions reduction projects
   - Green financing access (lower interest rates for sustainable projects)

**ESG Disclosure Requirements (Mexico):**

Under BMV (Mexican Stock Exchange) and increasingly for private companies:
- Annual emissions inventory (GRI 305)
- Climate risk assessment
- Reduction targets and progress
- Third-party verification recommended

**Real Example: Grupo Alfa (Monterrey)**
Their 2024 Sustainability Report includes:
- Scope 1+2 emissions: 2.1M tons CO2e (down 18% from 2020)
- Emissions intensity: 0.42 ton CO2e/$1M revenue (down 22%)
- Renewable energy: 38% of total consumption
- Investment in efficiency: $340M MXN (2020-2024)
- Savings from efficiency: $890M MXN over same period
- Published methodology and verified by KPMG
