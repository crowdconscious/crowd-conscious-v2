**The Global & Local Water Crisis:**

**Mexico's Water Reality:**

**3. Grey Water (Pollution)**

**Learning Content:**

**The 7 Types of Water Waste in Industry:**

**1. Leaks & Poor Maintenance**
- A 3mm hole leaks 900L/day
- A dripping tap wastes 15L/day
- Running toilets waste 200L/day
- Typical facility loses 15-25% of water to leaks

**Real Example: Danone Mexico**
- Implemented leak detection program
- Found leaks in 43% of production lines
- Saved 285,000 mÂ³/year after repairs
- ROI: 3.2 months (repair cost vs. savings)

**2. Single-Pass Cooling**
- Water used once then discarded
- Extremely wasteful (60-80% of industrial water use)
- Solution: Closed-loop cooling systems

**Cooling System Comparison:**
- Single-pass: 100,000 L/day
- Cooling tower (open loop): 5,000 L/day (95% savings)
- Closed-loop chiller: 500 L/day (99.5% savings)

**3. Inefficient Cleaning**
- High-pressure hoses instead of low-flow
- Constant running instead of intermittent
- No water recovery/reuse

**Better Cleaning Methods:**
- Dry cleaning first (sweep/vacuum), then wet
- Low-flow, high-pressure nozzles (70% less water)
- Fog/mist systems instead of hoses
- Clean-in-place (CIP) systems for food processing

**Real Example: CervecerÃ­a CuauhtÃ©moc Moctezuma**
- Switched from hose cleaning to automated CIP
- Reduced cleaning water by 65%
- Faster cleaning (30 min vs. 90 min)
- Better hygiene, less product loss

**4. Outdated Equipment**
- Old toilets: 13-19L per flush
- Old urinals: 4-6L per flush
- Old faucets: 12-15L per minute
- Old cooling systems: 10x+ more water than modern

**Upgrade Impact:**
- Dual-flush toilets: 3L or 6L (70% savings)
- Waterless urinals: 0L (100% savings)
- Sensor faucets: 1.9L per minute (87% savings)
- Payback: 1-3 years

**5. No Water Reuse/Recycling**
Most industrial facilities can reuse 30-70% of water:
- Cooling water can be used for cleaning
- Final rinse water can be used for initial rinse
- Condensate from HVAC can be captured
- Rainwater can be harvested

**Water Reuse Hierarchy:**
1. Direct reuse (same purpose, minor treatment)
2. Cascading use (clean â†’ less clean applications)
3. Treatment and reuse (for any purpose)
4. Groundwater recharge (long-term storage)

**Real Example: OXXO Distribution Center (Monterrey)**
During 2022 drought:
- Installed rainwater harvesting (10,000mÂ² roof)
- Captures 3,000mÂ³/year

- Total reuse: 5,000mÂ³/year (40% of consumption)
- Investment: $280,000 MXN
- Payback: 1.7 years

**6. Landscaping Waste**
- Non-native plants (high water needs)
- Midday watering (50% lost to evaporation)
- No drip irrigation
- No rainwater capture

**Solutions:**
- Xeriscape: Native, drought-tolerant plants (70-90% less water)
- Drip irrigation: 50% more efficient than sprinklers
- Smart controllers: Weather-based watering
- Mulch: Reduces evaporation 70%

**7. Process Inefficiency**
- More water used than technically necessary
- No optimization or innovation
- "We've always done it this way"

**Example: Electroplating Process Optimization**
- Traditional: 5 rinse tanks, 10,000L/day
- Optimized: 3 counterflow rinse tanks, 2,000L/day (80% savings)
- Investment: $150,000 MXN
- Annual savings: $180,000 MXN

**Wastewater Treatment & Community Water Access:**

**Why Wastewater Treatment Matters:**

In Mexico:
- 70% of industrial wastewater is untreated or poorly treated
- Polluted water costs $200B MXN/year in health and environment
- Companies causing pollution face increasing legal action

**Real Case: Quimicol (Puebla, 2021)**

- Contaminated local water supply, 5,000 people affected
- Company fined $50M MXN
- Required to install $80M MXN treatment plant
- CEO faced criminal charges
- Lesson: Cost of non-compliance >> cost of doing it right

**Treatment Technologies by Wastewater Type:**

**1. Light Contamination (cleaning water, sanitary)**
- **Primary treatment:** Settling, basic filtration
- **Secondary treatment:** Biological (bacteria eat organics)
- **Cost:** $50,000-200,000 MXN
- **Result:** Can reuse or safely discharge

**2. Medium Contamination (food processing, light chemicals)**
- **Activated sludge systems**
- **Membrane bioreactors (MBR)**
- **Cost:** $300,000-1,500,000 MXN
- **Result:** High-quality effluent suitable for reuse

**3. Heavy Contamination (chemical, metal finishing)**
- **Advanced oxidation**
- **Reverse osmosis**
- **Chemical treatment**
- **Cost:** $1M-5M MXN
- **Result:** Very clean water, can approach drinking water standards

**Real Example: Volkswagen Puebla**
- Treats 100% of facility wastewater
- Two treatment plants: 1.5 million L/day capacity
- Reuses 30% in processes
- Remaining discharge exceeds regulatory standards by 50%
- Worked with state government to help upgrade municipal system
- Investment: $120M MXN over 10 years
- Avoided costs: $25M MXN in fines, immeasurable reputation value

**Community Water Access Projects:**

**Option 1: Water Kiosks**

**Real Example: Bonafont (Water Company Paradox)**

**Option 2: School Water Programs**

**Option 3: Watershed Restoration**

**Real Example: Modelo (Beer Company, Multiple Locations)**
